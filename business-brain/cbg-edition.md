## Hook / opening

You've got the prompts. The templates. The bookmarked threads. The custom GPT you set up once and never opened again.

And you're more overwhelmed than before you started.

Here's why. You've been collecting tools. Nobody gave you the thing underneath them. So every time you sit down to use AI, you start from nothing. You re-explain who you are, what you sell, how you talk. Every single time.

That's not an AI problem. That's a setup problem. And it's fixable in an afternoon.

This isn't another folder of prompts to collect. It's one decision and one build. You pick the part of your business that's eating you alive. You give it a brain that already knows you. And you never re-explain yourself again.

One brain, built properly, beats eight half-built ones gathering dust. Especially if your actual brain hates a half-finished thing as much as mine does.

## What a "brain" actually is (no jargon)

Forget "agent". Here's the plain version.

A brain is a saved space where AI already knows your business before you say a word. Your voice. Your offers. Your rules. What you'd never say. Who you're talking to.

You set it up once. After that, every time you open it, it starts from who you actually are. Not from a blank box that makes you sound like everyone else.

On Claude you build this as a Project. On the free plan you paste a short brief at the top instead. Same result. I'll show you both. You do not need to be technical. If you can copy and paste, you can do this.

## The 5 brains your business runs on

You already run all five. You've just never named them. That's the problem. Unnamed work is the work that falls on the floor.

**1. Content brain.** Says the thing, in your voice. Captions, emails, scripts. The one most people build first because it's the one screaming loudest.

**2. Sales brain.** Helps the right person say yes. Sales pages, launch emails, the soft-sell post that doesn't make you feel gross.

**3. Client brain.** Holds the experience. Welcome messages, session prep, the boundary email you've been avoiding, the follow-up you keep forgetting.

**4. Strategy brain.** A thinking partner that actually knows your capacity. Decisions, launches, the "should I even do this" gut-check. The one that talks you out of the shiny thing.

**5. Visibility brain.** Gets you seen without the cringe. Podcast pitches, your bio, the collab message you've drafted nine times and never sent.

You don't build all five today. You build one. The right one. The next page tells you which.

## Which brain to build first (the honest audit)

Don't pick the exciting one. Pick the one that's costing you the most right now.

For each brain, answer two questions:
- How much of my week does this eat?
- How consistent is what comes out, honestly?

The one that eats the most time and comes out different every time? That's your first brain. That's the one screaming for help.

If everything feels equal, build the **Content brain** first. It's the most repetitive, it shows results fastest, and a quick win is exactly what an overwhelmed brain needs to believe the rest is worth doing.

Write your pick here: _______________

That's the whole decision. You're not building an empire. You're giving one exhausting job a brain. Start there.

## The 6 things every brain needs

A brain without these is just a chatbot. With them, it's a part of your team that turns up the same every time.

1. **Who it is.** Its job. What it decides on its own, what it checks with you first.
2. **What it knows.** Your voice, your offers, your past work. The stuff it references so you never paste your brand guide again.
3. **How you sound.** The actual rules. Your banned words. Your rhythm. The line between "sounds like me" and "sounds like LinkedIn".
4. **Who it's for.** Your reader, described by where they're stuck, not their job title.
5. **What it must never do.** Your guardrails. The hill it dies on. (Mine: no em dashes, no hustle language, never sound like a brand talking to a market.)
6. **How it talks to you.** Drafting mode or editing mode. When to run free, when to be precise.

You'll fill these in for your first brain on the next page. Rough notes are fine. Honesty beats polish here. A vague brain gives you vague output that sounds like everyone else.

## Build your first brain (the template)

Copy this. Fill in the brackets. Paste it into a Claude Project, or at the top of a chat if you're on free. This is the whole setup.

```text
You are the [CONTENT / SALES / CLIENT / STRATEGY / VISIBILITY] brain for
[YOUR NAME], who helps [WHO YOU HELP] do [THE TRANSFORMATION, not the job title].

WHO I AM:
- What I do, in one specific sentence: [a transformation, not "I'm a coach"]
- What makes my approach different: [the thing only you do]
- Proof: [years, results, the moment that earns trust]

HOW I SOUND:
- Tone in 3 to 5 words: [...]
- My rhythm: [short and punchy? do you swear? do you open with empathy?]
- Words I will never use: [your banned list — the jargon that makes you wince]
- Always: contractions, second person, prose not bullet-dumps.
- Every piece reads like a real human who knows their stuff. Never AI.

WHO I'M FOR:
- My person, by where they're stuck: [not "entrepreneurs" — which ones, mid-what]
- Their own words for the problem: [how THEY describe it, not how you'd brand it]
- What turns them off: [the language that makes them close the tab]

WHAT I OFFER:
- Free thing: [name, link]
- Paid thing: [name, who it's for, link]
- How I ask: permission-based. "If this lands..." "Want me to..." Never fake urgency, never countdown-timer panic.

HOW TO WORK WITH ME:
- When I want a draft: give me a full first go, don't over-ask. I'll shape it.
- When I want an edit: I'll paste my own words. Be precise, change only what's weak.
- If output drifts generic: tell me, and start fresh in the same space.
```

That's it. That's the brain. The setup is the work. Everything after this is just asking.

## The prompt library (in your voice, ready to use)

Built for the Content brain (your first one). Copy, fill the brackets, paste inside your brain. Read it back. Refine in the same chat, don't start over. It holds context. Build on it.

**Value post.** Write a [platform] post about [topic]. For [who it's for, by where they're stuck]. Goal: [seen / replies / quiet nudge to the free thing]. Open by naming the thing they're carrying. No "are you tired of". Say one thing, clearly. End on something real, not a performed CTA. My voice, my rules.

**Permission post (the honest truth one).** Write a [platform] post that says the thing most people in my space won't. The truth: [the uncomfortable thing you actually believe]. Why it matters to them: [...]. First line stops the scroll. No hedging. Take the position. Warm, not aggressive.

**Behind-the-scenes.** Write a [platform] post telling the real story of [moment]. The lesson underneath: [...]. Open mid-story, no throat-clearing, no "I want to share something". Let it breathe. Land the lesson without spelling it out. This is a human moment, not a teaching post.

**Email that sounds like you typed it.** Write an email about [topic]. To [who]. Open with a story or a straight observation, never "I hope this finds you well". One idea. Soft bridge to [free or paid thing] only if it earns its place. Sign off like a voice note someone typed up. 200 to 300 words.

**The self-check (run this on anything before it goes out).** Read that back. Does it sound like me talking to a mate, or like AI? Flag every generic phrase. Check it against my banned words. Where's it too long? Cut 20 percent without losing the point.

## The proof: a real brain, running (mine)

Most people teaching this hand you an empty template and wish you luck. I'm going to show you one that's actually built, because seeing a real one makes the blank version far less scary.

This is my Content brain. The thing I write Calm Back posts through. I'm not going to pretend the whole machine is finished, because it isn't, and I'd never sell you that. What's real is the part that matters most: the voice setup. Here's what's actually in mine.

**Who it's for, in my brain's own words.** Not "small business owners". It's: women hanging on by their teeth, carrying too much, who think they need a rebrand when what they need is someone to tell them what they've built is already worth something. That single line changes every post it writes.

**My banned words, written down and enforced.** Transform. Empower. Unlock. Elevate. Journey-as-metaphor. Level up. Game changer. My brain scans every draft against that list and cuts every match before I ever see it. That's why nothing it writes sounds like a LinkedIn motivational poster.

**My actual rhythm, captured from how I really talk.** Short sentences. One idea each. Starts with "And" sometimes. Swears when it fits, never when it's trying to be edgy. Says the thing, doesn't circle it. I pulled this from my own voice notes, not from how I think I sound. That gap matters.

**Two gatekeeper questions it asks before it writes a word.** "Why should someone give a fuck about this?" and "Could anyone else have written this, or only me?" If a post can't answer both, the brain tells me it's not ready instead of polishing something hollow. That's the difference between a brain and a content mill.

**A read-aloud test at the end.** Every draft gets checked: would I say this out loud without cringing? If not, rewrite.

Here's the honest part. The bit that takes the work is not the prompts. It's the half-hour I spent being truthful about my voice, my person, and my rules. The brain is only as good as that. I'm showing you mine so you know exactly what "done properly" looks like. Yours won't be the same. It'll be yours. That's the whole point.

What I haven't finished: the scheduling, the auto-logging, the full filming-day pipeline. I'm telling you that on purpose. You do not need the whole machine to get the win. You need the voice setup. Build that, and you're already most of the way there.

## Getting more out of it

**Stay in the chat.** Output not right? Don't start over. Say "tone's right, opening's too soft, make the first line bolder". It holds the thread. Use it.

**Make it think, not just write.** "Be my strategy brain for a second. Here's the decision: [...]. Don't cheerlead me. Tell me what fits, what conflicts, and what I'm not seeing." A brain that only agrees with you is useless.

**One brain at a time.** Got the Content brain running and sounding like you? Now build the next. Same six things, same template, different job. You're not starting from scratch. You're copying a system that already works.

You don't need more tools. You need one part of your business to stop starting from zero.

Build that. The rest follows.
